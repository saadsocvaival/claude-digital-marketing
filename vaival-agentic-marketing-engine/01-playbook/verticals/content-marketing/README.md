# Content Marketing Vertical

## Charter
Content Marketing is the voice of the company in the market and the fuel that powers every other channel — SEO needs content to rank, Paid needs LPs and ad copy, Email needs nurture, Social needs shareable assets (§11.1). At mid-size, the discipline is **fewer, better assets** rather than high-volume output. Quality beats cadence: 4 exceptional articles per month beats 20 mediocre ones (§11.6).

## Team Roles + Tier (§11.2)
| Role | Tier | Headcount |
|---|---|---|
| Content Marketing Manager | Tier 2 (Vertical Lead) | 1 |
| Senior Content Writer (SaaS) | Tier 3 | 1–2 |
| Graphic Designer | Tier 3 | 1 |
| Video / Multimedia Producer | Tier 3 | 1 |

Department Head: `head-of-content`.

## KPIs (§11.5)
- Content pieces published / month — quality-gated, per OKR.
- Organic traffic from content — MoM growth, GA4.
- Content-sourced MQLs — % of total MQL mix, GA4 + CRM.
- Avg time on page (long-form) — ≥3 min.
- Case studies published / quarter — minimum 1–2.
- Content refresh rate — no more than 20% of top-50 pages older than 18 months without update.

## Weekly Cadence
- Editorial stand-up: brief queue, draft queue, edit queue, publish queue.
- SEO + Content alignment session: confirm next briefs.
- Social + Paid asset requests intake (5-business-day SLA on visuals).
- Performance check on most-recent publishes (30 / 90-day check from §11.4).
- Repurposing pass — confirm each major asset spawned ≥4 derivative formats (§11.6).

## Monthly Cadence
- Editorial calendar lock for next month (with SEO + Social + Paid).
- Refresh queue: identify top-50 pages aging out of 18-month window.
- Content-sourced MQL contribution review with Analytics-Ops.
- Funnel-stage balance review across TOFU/MOFU/BOFU/Retention pillars (§11.3).
- Quarterly: case-study production cycle (≥1–2 published).

## Policies + Thresholds
- **Quality beats cadence** (§11.6) — refuse to publish to volume target if rubric <8.
- **Research before writing** — Surfer/Clearscope coverage validated before draft.
- **One CTA per piece** — funnel-stage aligned (§11.6).
- **Customer language first** — harvest from support tickets, sales call transcripts, G2/Capterra reviews.
- **Cite primary sources only** — no second-hand statistics.
- **Repurpose mandate** — every major asset → blog + email + 3 social posts + short-video script (§11.6).
- **Tiered approval (§11.4 step 7)**: Tier 1 (Vertical Lead sign-off) for standard pieces; Tier 2 (Head of Marketing) for thought-leadership / brand-claim / executive byline pieces.
- **Internal linking on every publish**: ≥3 outbound, ≥2 inbound (cross-vertical with SEO).

## Refusal / Escalation Triggers
- Plagiarism / undisclosed AI mass-generation → hard refuse.
- Fabricated statistics or unverifiable claims → refuse, escalate to brand+legal.
- Executive-byline copy → HITL the named executive before publish.
- Competitor attacks / negative comparison content not vetted by legal → HITL.
- Customer-quoted content without written approval → HITL CS / customer.
- Refresh that materially changes positioning → HITL Head of Marketing.

## Output Artifacts Produced
- Briefs (handed off from SEO vertical, owned through publish).
- Drafts, designed assets, video scripts, repurposed derivatives.
- Editorial calendar (rolling 90 days).
- 30 / 90-day performance reads + refresh recommendations.
- Case studies, whitepapers, lead-magnet content.
