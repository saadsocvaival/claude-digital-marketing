# Email Sequence — Enterprise Nurture (MQL → SQL)

Goal: lift MQL→SQL CVR by +15% on enterprise-fit leads who requested TCO calc or comparison content but didn't book a demo.

Entry: fit ≥70, intent 30–60, engagement ≥20. Exit: demo booked OR 28d OR unsubscribe. Suppression: existing customers, competitor domains.

## E1 — T+0 — "Here's your TCO calc with your numbers"
- Subject: "Your Loopgate TCO estimate — {eng-count} engineers"
- From: AE
- Body: Pre-calculated TCO vs LD and homegrown for their company size (enriched). One link to detailed calc.
- CTA: See the detailed math.

## E2 — T+3 days — "The question SOC2 auditors asked Fintech X"
- Subject: "What SOC2 auditors ask about feature flags (real examples)"
- Body: 4 specific audit questions + how Loopgate answers each. Link to compliance page.
- CTA: Download the SOC2 readiness checklist.

## E3 — T+7 days — "Side-by-side with LaunchDarkly"
- Subject: "Loopgate vs LaunchDarkly — the comparison we run on our own site"
- Body: 6-row comparison (not 30 — focused). Link to full page.
- CTA: Run the comparison.

## E4 — T+10 days — Case study matched to industry
- Subject: "How a {industry} team moved from LD in 4 days"
- Body: Specific case from their vertical (fintech/marketplace/digital-health). Migration outcome, audit outcome, cost outcome.
- CTA: Read the case.

## E5 — T+14 days — Human outreach from AE
- Subject: "30 minutes — worth it?"
- Body: 3 lines. "I'm not pushing a demo. If the TCO math lands, let's skip the pitch and talk migration. Here's 3 times next week."
- CTA: Pick a slot (Chili Piper link).

## E6 — T+21 days — Soft close
- Subject: "Keeping this short"
- Body: "If Loopgate is wrong for you right now, that's fine. If it's timing — I'll check back in Q3. If it's fit — I'd love to hear why, off the record. Reply with which."
- CTA: Reply.

## E7 — T+28 days — Exit (friendly)
- Subject: "Moving you off this sequence"
- Body: 2 lines. Link to newsletter if they want brand updates without outreach. Unsub preserved.

## Measurement
- Primary: SQL rate lift vs control (+15%).
- Secondary: meeting-booked rate, unsubscribe rate (guardrail ≤0.8%/email).
- Attribution: multi-touch; this sequence credited at influenced + last-touch.
