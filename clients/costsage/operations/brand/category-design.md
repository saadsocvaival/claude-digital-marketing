# Category Design Worksheet — CostSage

> Framework: Play Bigger ("Play Bigger: How Pirates, Dreamers, and Innovators Create and Dominate Markets" — Ramadan, Peterson, Lochhead, Maney).
> Purpose: Define the category CostSage intends to design and own. The category is the asset; the product is downstream.

---

## 1. Why a New Category?

The "Cloud Cost Management" category is twelve years old and structurally a **dashboard category**. It rewards visibility (CloudZero, Vantage), allocation (CloudHealth, Apptio), or narrow automation (ProsperOps for commitments, nOps for rightsizing). None of them ship the work. All of them require a human FinOps practitioner to translate insight into engineering tickets.

CostSage is not a better dashboard. It is the first **agentic FinOps operator** — software that does the work a FinOps engineer would do, with human approval at the action boundary.

That is a different category. We name it, frame it, and own it.

## 4. POV Statement (1 paragraph)

The first generation of FinOps tools gave engineering and finance teams visibility — dashboards, allocation models, anomaly alerts. Visibility is not savings. Every dollar of cloud waste eliminated still requires a human to write a Jira ticket, schedule a maintenance window, and ship the change. That bottleneck is the entire category. Agentic FinOps removes it. An AI agent reasons over the bill, plans the action, and executes the change with a human approval at the trigger. The dashboard era taught teams what to do. The agentic era does it.

## 5. The Problem We Frame (and the Enemy We Name)

| Element | CostSage Position |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | Cloud waste is 20–35% of spend, and FinOps tooling tells you about it instead of fixing it. |
| **Enemy** | The dashboard era of FinOps — "visibility-only" platforms that turn cost work into permanent backlog. |
| **Villain attributes** | Read-only. Human-bound. Slow. Engineer-tax-heavy. |
| **Hero attributes** | Acts. Approves. Rolls back. Reasons. Audit-trailed. |
| **The shift** | From *seeing* waste to *closing the loop* on waste. |

We never name CloudZero, nOps, Vantage, or ProsperOps in copy. We name the *category* they share.

## 8. Naming Hygiene

When writing about the category:
- ✅ "Agentic FinOps" (capitalized like a proper noun on first reference, lowercase after).
- ✅ "FinOps operator" (the role/product noun).
- ❌ "AI FinOps" (commoditized, generic).
- ❌ "FinOps copilot" (we are explicitly not a copilot — see voice guidelines).
- ❌ "Autonomous FinOps" (triggers CFO safety objection).
