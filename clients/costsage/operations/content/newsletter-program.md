# Newsletter Program — "Bill of Materials"

> Bi-weekly newsletter. Working name: **Bill of Materials** (alts: *The Idle Compute*, *Closing the Loop*).
> Owner: Content Lead • v1.0 • 2026-04-28

---

## 1. Why this newsletter exists

CostSage's primary paid acquisition is expensive (FinOps keywords are competitive). Owned audience compounds. The newsletter is the highest-leverage owned asset because:

- It is the only V5 channel that arrives in the buyer's inbox without an algorithm in between.
- It is the natural distribution layer for whitepaper chapter excerpts, the comparison pieces, and customer stories.
- It is the proof that we have a category point of view, issued every two weeks.

**Goal at 12 months:** 7,500 subscribers, 38% open rate, 6% click rate, 8% of trial signups attributable to newsletter touch.

## 2. Naming

**Selected:** *Bill of Materials*
- Engineering pun (BOM is a manufacturing term repurposed for engineering) — operator-coded.
- Says exactly what it is — a teardown of a cloud bill.
- No "FinOps" in the name — not all readers self-identify with the term.

Alternatives kept in reserve:
- *The Idle Compute* — wittier, narrower.
- *Closing the Loop* — category-aligned, slightly more abstract.

## 4. Cadence and length

- **Cadence:** Bi-weekly, Tuesday 9am ET.
- **Length:** 600–900 words body. Read in 4 minutes.
- **Schedule:** issue n drafted by Wednesday week n-1, reviewed Thursday, scheduled Friday.

## 5. The 5-Block Template

Every issue uses these five blocks, in order. No exceptions.

### Block 1 — Editor's Note (60–100 words)
- "I" voice. One observation from the last two weeks of FinOps practice.
- Anchor for the rest of the issue.

### Block 2 — The Teardown (250–400 words)
- The main piece. One specific cost lesson with numbers.
- Either a fresh teardown or a sharpened excerpt of a pillar piece.
- One image / chart maximum.

### Block 3 — One Number (40–80 words)
- A single statistic from CostSage's own data or public sources.
- Format: "[Number] [unit per timeframe] — [one sentence of context]."
- Example: "$583/day — average waste on a $50K/mo AWS bill in Q1 2026."

### Block 4 — Three Links (≤120 words)
- Three external reads from the FinOps / cloud / engineering world.
- Plus optionally one internal link (CostSage blog or whitepaper) — never more than one self-link in this block.

### Block 5 — The Ask (60–100 words)
- One soft CTA. Rotate across:
  - Trial CostSage
  - Forward to a colleague
  - Reply with your worst-cost-leak war story (we publish the best ones with permission)
  - Download the playbook

## 6. Subject-line conventions

- ≤55 characters.
- Lead with a number when possible.
- No emoji.
- Examples: "The $583/day every $50K AWS bill loses", "RI vs SP in 2026: when each one wins", "What an agent sees in 60 seconds".
