# Employee Advocacy Program

Goal: every CostSage employee is a credible, low-pressure amplifier for company content. Authentic > polished. Optional, not mandatory.

---

## Principles

1. **Voluntary.** Nobody is required to post.
2. **Authentic.** Employees post in their own voice, not company-branded boilerplate.
3. **Substance > frequency.** One thoughtful post/month beats 4 reshares.
4. **Disclose.** "I work on CostSage" goes in profile bio at minimum.
5. **No quotas.** Track engagement *opportunities offered*, not posts produced.

---

## Suggested cadence (per employee)

| Activity | Cadence | Effort |
|---|---|---|
| Update LinkedIn headline to mention CostSage | Once | 2 min |
| Reshare 1 company post / month | Monthly | 1 min |
| Comment on 1 company post / week | Weekly | 2 min |
| Original post about something they shipped or learned | Quarterly (suggested) | 30 min |

---

## Content menu (pick what fits)

### Easy lifts (5–10 min)
- Reshare the company carousel with 1-line opinion.
- Comment substantively on the founder's post (not just "great post!").
- Quote-amplify a teammate's milestone.

### Medium (30 min)
- Write a "what I learned this week" post tied to recent work (without revealing customer/IP).
- Share a snippet of an internal doc (non-confidential) you found useful.
- Reply to a category thread with technical insight.

### Heavier (1–2 hours)
- Original technical post (e.g., "How we ship savings PRs" engineering write-up).
- Speak at a meetup / record a short Loom we publish.

---

## Do list

- Talk about the *problem space* (cloud cost, FinOps, AWS economics) more than the product.
- Share opinions, even mild contrarian ones — they get engagement.
- Engage with prospect/customer comments on company posts.
- Flag wins to marketing for amplification (`#advocacy` channel in Slack [TBD-OPERATOR]).
- Tag CostSage when you mention it (gives the company page reach signal).

## Don't list

- Don't share confidential customer info — *never*, anonymized or not, without sign-off.
- Don't slam competitors. Even gentle mockery reads as small.
- Don't post unfounded numbers ("we save customers 80%!"). Use sourced figures.
- Don't link-bait or rage-bait — burns trust.
- Don't post the same content across 3 channels verbatim — feels like a campaign.
- Don't engage with trolls. Mute, move on.

---

## Onboarding (first 2 weeks, new hire)

Day 1: brief on this program. Hand them the link.
Day 3: optional 30-min coffee with marketing — voice, comfort, what to avoid.
Day 7: opt-in form for advocacy. No pressure if they pass.
Day 14: if opted in, give them: bio template, 1 reshare, 1 founder post to comment on.
