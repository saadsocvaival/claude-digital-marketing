How We Write at CostSage

This is the public version of our brand-voice guide. Every page on costsage.ai is checked against the rules below before publish. We share it because we'd rather you write to us than wonder how we'd write to you.

Brand essence

Agentic, Not Dashboard. We're the platform that closes the visibility-to-action loop on cloud cost. We talk like engineers who got tired of reports that don't ship savings. Direct. Honest. Specific. Numerate.

Five tone axes (do / don't)

AxisDoDon't
Confident, not boastful"We rightsize EC2 with one-click rollback.""World's #1 cloud cost optimization platform."
Specific, not aspirational"$61K/month average savings on confirmed cohorts" (with citation when public)."Save big!" / "Massive savings!"
Plain, not jargony"We watch your idle EC2 instances.""Synergistic AI-driven workload-rebalancing."
Honest, not hedge-everything"Vantage publishes pricing; that's a positive signal.""While CostSage may possibly potentially help in some scenarios..."
Engineering-first, not sales-first"Approval gates + audit trails + rollback.""Best-in-class governance for the modern enterprise."

Vocabulary

Preferred words

agent · execute · under approval · audit trail · rightsize · idle · tagging governance · rollback · cohort · per public docs · Verified · Per public docs · Unknown

Banned words

synergy · leverage (as a verb) · best-in-class · cutting-edge · game-changing · revolutionary · seamlessly · holistic · turnkey · solutions (as in "our solutions"). If a word would be at home on a 2014 enterprise software brochure, don't use it.

Sentence-level rules

  • Length: aim for 12–22 words per sentence. Vary; don't make every sentence the same length.
  • Active voice: "CostSage rightsizes your EC2." Not "EC2 instances are rightsized by CostSage."
  • Numbers over adjectives: "$61K/month" beats "significant savings."
  • Hedge for honesty, not for cover: "Per public docs" is fine. "May potentially possibly" is hiding.
  • One claim per sentence. Don't stack three.

POV stance

We write in first-person plural ("we") for company copy and second-person ("you") for product copy. Founder posts use first-person singular. We don't use the royal "CostSage" as the subject of every sentence — sometimes "your AWS bill" or "your engineering team" is the subject, and that's better.

Sample rewrites

Before

"Leveraging cutting-edge AI synergies, our world-class FinOps solution delivers seamless cost optimization across the modern cloud-native ecosystem."

After

"CostSage's agents identify rightsizing, idle-resource, and Reserved Instance opportunities on AWS and Azure — and execute them under your approval, with audit trails."

Before

"Best-in-class enterprise-grade automation that holistically transforms your cloud spend management."

After

"We close the loop between visibility and action. The agent finds waste, you approve the fix, the agent executes it, and you have an audit trail at the end."

Why we publish this

If a vendor can't articulate how they write, they're probably writing the same brochure as their competitors. We'd rather you read our pages and recognise the voice — even before you see the logo.
