# Category Design — {Client}

**Frameworks:** Play Bigger (Ramadan/Peterson/Lochhead) + April Dunford positioning hybrid.

## 1. Category POV
- **Problem we name:** {1 sentence — the problem no one else is naming this way}
- **Old-world assumption we reject:** {what the market believes that we say is wrong}
- **New-world assumption we assert:** {our contrarian claim}
- **Why now:** {tech / regulatory / cultural shift making this inevitable}

## 2. Category name
- **Proposed category:** {noun phrase, 2–4 words}
- **One-liner definition:** {so a peer could repeat it after hearing once}
- **Adjacent categories we're NOT:** {what we are explicitly distinct from}

## 3. King-making strategy
- **Lightning-strike moment:** {the anchor event / asset / launch that claims the category}
- **Thought-leadership cadence:** {weekly/monthly owned POV drumbeat}
- **Analyst/press POV seeding:** {named analysts, target outlets, 90-day plan}
- **Community build:** {event, Slack, podcast — the gathering place}

## 4. Proof of category
- **Reference customers:** {3–5 logos willing to tell the category story}
- **Category-defining metric:** {the KPI only our category measures}
- **Ecosystem partners:** {who else needs this category to exist}

## 5. Anti-patterns
- Category-of-one claims without evidence (laughable)
- "AI-powered X" — technology is not a category
- Re-naming an existing category (you're just a feature)

## 6. Gating
- Category claim requires: ≥2 reference customers + 1 analyst mention + 1 paid customer outside original ICP + signs of imitation by competitors.
- Until then: position *into* an existing category with a sharpened POV.
